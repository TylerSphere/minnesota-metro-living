You have narrowed it down to two suburbs. Both show up on every "best places to live in Minnesota" list. Both have highly rated schools. Both are west of Minneapolis with reasonable commutes downtown.

And yet Edina and Eden Prairie are not the same place. The families who thrive in one do not always thrive in the other. The homes, the neighborhoods, the daily rhythms, and the price tags tell very different stories.

This is the comparison you actually need. Not the rankings. Not the marketing. The real differences that will shape your daily life, your commute, your kids' education, and your monthly mortgage payment.

If you are relocating to the Twin Cities and trying to decide between these two suburbs, this guide will help you make the right call.

## The 30-Second Version: What Makes Each City Different

Edina is an established, inner-ring suburb with older neighborhoods, walkable commercial districts, and a reputation as the most prestigious address in the Twin Cities metro. The median home price sits around **$625,000**, and many properties push well past $1 million. The trade-off for that prestige is smaller lots, older housing stock in many areas, and property taxes that reflect the premium location.

Eden Prairie is a larger, more sprawling suburb that developed primarily in the 1980s and 1990s. It offers more home for your money, with a median price around **$485,000**. The trade-off is a longer commute to downtown Minneapolis, less walkability in most neighborhoods, and a more car-dependent lifestyle.

Both have excellent schools. Both have strong community identities. But the day-to-day experience of living in each place is meaningfully different.

## Schools: The Numbers Behind the Reputation

For families with children, schools are often the deciding factor. Both districts perform well above state averages, but they are structured differently and serve different populations.

**Edina Public Schools** operates as a single high school district. Every student in the city eventually attends Edina High School, which consistently ranks in the **top 5 public high schools in Minnesota** based on graduation rates, test scores, and college placement. The district enrolls approximately 8,500 students across 10 schools. The concentrated model means families share a common experience, and the high school carries significant resources and programming depth.

**Eden Prairie Schools** is larger, serving roughly 9,200 students. The district feeds into Eden Prairie High School, which also ranks among the state's top performers. Eden Prairie has invested heavily in career and technical education programs, and the high school offers one of the more comprehensive STEM tracks in the metro.

The practical difference for families: Edina's single-track system means every neighborhood feeds into the same schools. In Eden Prairie, which elementary and middle school your child attends depends on where you live, and the experience can vary by neighborhood.

Both districts consistently outperform state averages on standardized assessments. Edina's ACT composite average hovers around **25.8**, while Eden Prairie's sits at approximately **25.2**. The gap is marginal. The cultural difference is more significant: Edina's schools carry a longer legacy reputation, while Eden Prairie's have earned their standing through consistent recent performance.

## Home Prices and What You Actually Get

The price gap between these two suburbs is real and substantial.

In Edina, the **median sale price in 2026 is approximately $625,000**. Entry-level homes in less central neighborhoods start around $425,000, but these are typically 1950s-era ramblers that need updating. Move-in ready family homes in desirable neighborhoods like Countryside or Indian Hills regularly list above $800,000. Larger properties near the lakes or in South Edina can exceed $1.5 million.

In Eden Prairie, the **median sale price is approximately $485,000**. That same budget that buys a dated rambler in Edina buys a 4-bedroom, 3-bath home built in the 1990s with a finished basement and a two-car garage in Eden Prairie. The typical Eden Prairie home offers **30-40% more square footage** than a comparably priced Edina property.

The question is what you are buying with the Edina premium. You get a shorter commute to downtown Minneapolis. You get smaller lot sizes but more mature landscaping and established neighborhoods. You get walkable access to the 50th and France commercial district or the shops at Southdale. You get the Edina address, which carries weight in certain professional and social circles in the Twin Cities.

Whether that premium is worth it depends entirely on what you prioritize.

## The Commute: Where You Work Matters

If you work in downtown Minneapolis, Edina is closer. The drive from central Edina to the IDS Center takes approximately **18-25 minutes** without traffic, or 25-40 minutes during rush hour. You also have access to Metro Transit bus routes and can connect to the Blue Line light rail at the Mall of America if you are willing to add a transfer.

Eden Prairie is farther west. The drive to downtown runs **25-35 minutes** without traffic, stretching to 40-55 minutes during peak hours. The Southwest Light Rail line now connects Eden Prairie to downtown Minneapolis, with the **Eden Prairie Town Center station** offering direct service to the Warehouse District and US Bank Stadium. The full ride takes approximately 45 minutes, but for commuters who prefer to avoid driving, it changes the calculation.

If you work in the western suburbs, the picture flips. Eden Prairie sits in the heart of the I-494 corridor, with quick access to major employers like **UnitedHealth Group, Optum, C.H. Robinson, and Cargill's headquarters in Wayzata**. Eden Prairie also borders Minnetonka and Hopkins, making it central to the western suburban job market.

Edina positions you well for employers along the 494 strip too, but adds easier access to South Minneapolis, Richfield, and the airport corridor.

The honest answer: if you work downtown, Edina saves you time. If you work in the western suburbs, Eden Prairie is more central. If you work remotely, the commute math matters less than the lifestyle fit.

## Lifestyle and Daily Life

Edina feels older and denser. The city developed earlier, and many neighborhoods have a walkable, established character. The **50th and France district** offers locally owned shops, restaurants, and a farmers market that draws visitors from across the metro. **Centennial Lakes Park** provides a central gathering spot with walking paths, paddle boats, and outdoor concerts in summer.

The housing stock in much of Edina dates to the 1950s and 1960s. Ramblers and split-levels dominate. Many have been extensively renovated, but the bones are old. Lot sizes are smaller than what you find in outer-ring suburbs.

Eden Prairie feels more spread out and more suburban in the classic sense. The city covers **35 square miles** compared to Edina's 16. Neighborhoods are organized around cul-de-sacs and green spaces rather than walkable commercial cores. **Eden Prairie Center** provides shopping and dining, but you will drive there rather than walk.

What Eden Prairie offers is space and access to nature. The city maintains over **170 miles of trails** and more than 2,500 acres of parkland. **Bryant Lake Regional Park** and the **Minnesota River Bluffs** provide wilderness-adjacent recreation without leaving the city limits. For families who prioritize outdoor activity, Eden Prairie delivers more immediate access to trails, lakes, and open space.

The honest trade-off: Edina is for people who want an urban-adjacent, walkable suburb with established neighborhoods and a prestige address. Eden Prairie is for people who want more space, newer homes, and easy access to outdoor recreation.

## Property Taxes and the True Cost of Ownership

Both cities have above-average property tax rates compared to the broader metro, but Edina's are notably higher.

In Edina, the effective property tax rate runs approximately **1.15-1.25%** of estimated market value. On a $625,000 home, that translates to roughly **$7,200-$7,800 per year** in property taxes.

In Eden Prairie, the effective rate runs approximately **1.05-1.15%**. On a $485,000 home, annual property taxes come in around **$5,100-$5,600**.

The combination of higher home prices and higher tax rates means the total cost of ownership in Edina can exceed Eden Prairie by **$30,000-$50,000 annually** when you factor in mortgage payments, taxes, and insurance. Over a 10-year period, that difference compounds significantly.

This is not a reason to avoid Edina. It is a reason to understand what you are buying and whether the location premium aligns with your priorities and budget.

## What Each City Does Better

**Edina does these things better:**

Walkability. The 50th and France area and Grandview give residents genuine walkable neighborhoods with shops, restaurants, and community gathering spots.

Commute to downtown Minneapolis. If you work in the city core, Edina saves 10-20 minutes each way compared to Eden Prairie.

Established, mature neighborhoods. The tree-lined streets and landscaping in Edina's older neighborhoods have a character that newer suburbs cannot replicate.

**Eden Prairie does these things better:**

Value per dollar. Your housing budget stretches 30-40% further in Eden Prairie than in Edina.

Outdoor recreation. The trail network and park system in Eden Prairie are more extensive and more integrated into daily life.

Newer housing stock. Most Eden Prairie homes were built between 1975 and 2005, meaning fewer major system replacements and more modern floor plans.

Light rail access. The Southwest Light Rail line provides Eden Prairie residents with a direct transit option to downtown that Edina lacks.
