# WHO WE ARE TALKING TO

This file defines the Ideal Reader Profile for all content on Minnesota Metro Living.
Every SEO blog post, GEO page, city guide, and news article should be written
with one of these two people in mind.

The conversion goal for both: connect with a Pemberton Real Estate agent
when they are ready to buy or sell.

---

# READER 1: THE RELOCATING BUYER

## Who They Are

Someone who is moving to the Minnesota metro — from another state, another city,
or another country — and is in research mode. They are trying to figure out
which suburb or city fits their life before they commit to anything.

They are not experts on the Twin Cities. They are smart people trying to
get smart quickly about an unfamiliar market. They trust specific, honest
information over polished marketing language.

## Demographics

- **Age:** 28–52
- **Household:** Often a couple or family with children; sometimes a solo professional
- **Reason for moving:** Job relocation (3M, Target, Best Buy, Cargill, healthcare systems),
  remote work flexibility, family reasons, retirement, or cost-of-living migration from
  higher-cost metros (Chicago, Seattle, Denver, NYC)
- **Income:** $80,000–$200,000+ household
- **Home budget:** $350,000–$750,000
- **Timeline:** 1–9 months from decision to purchase

## Psychographics

**They believe:**
- The right suburb will make or break how much they love their new life
- School quality is the single most important filter if they have children
- Commute time is a real quality-of-life issue, not just a number
- They need to talk to someone who actually lives there, not a website with stock photos

**They feel:**
- Overwhelmed by the number of suburbs and how similar they sound from the outside
- Nervous about making a $500K+ decision in a market they don't know
- Relieved when they find content that is specific and honest, not just promotional
- Impatient with content that doesn't give them the actual answer

**They want:**
- Straight comparisons: Edina vs Eden Prairie, Woodbury vs Maple Grove
- Real talk about winters, schools, commutes, and cost of living
- To understand the personality of each city, not just the stats
- To find a trustworthy local expert before they make the trip

**They are NOT:**
- People who have lived in Minnesota their whole life and already know the suburbs
- People who are just casually curious with no move in their future
- Investors looking for rental yield analysis

## The Trigger Moment

- Job offer accepted, housing search begins
- Company announces office relocation, family needs to decide in 60 days
- Sold their home in another state, looking at Minnesota as a destination
- Remote work approval received, realizing they can finally afford a better life somewhere else

## What They've Already Done

- Googled "best suburbs Minneapolis" and gotten listicles with no depth
- Looked at Zillow and Realtor.com but have no context for what they're seeing
- Asked coworkers or friends who might know someone in the area
- Visited the metro once or twice but didn't have time to go deep on neighborhoods

# READER 2: THE WITHIN-METRO MOVER

## Who They Are

Someone already living in the Twin Cities metro who is planning a move within it.
They're upgrading, downsizing, moving from an apartment to a home,
or moving between cities for schools, space, or lifestyle reasons.

They know Minnesota. They need to know a specific market better
than they currently do.

## Demographics

- **Age:** 25–60
- **Household:** Wide range — first-time buyers, growing families, empty nesters
- **Current situation:** Renter ready to buy, homeowner upgrading, or homeowner
  downsizing after kids leave
- **Income:** $70,000–$180,000
- **Home budget:** $300,000–$600,000

## Psychographics

**They believe:**
- They've outgrown where they are and the next move needs to be the right one
- The difference between a good and great neighborhood matters more
  when you have kids in school
- They should probably be working with a local agent who knows the specific
  pocket they're looking in

**They feel:**
- Like they need to do more research before calling an agent
- Uncertain about which city offers the best value right now
- Slightly anxious about timing the market

**They want:**
- Specific, data-informed comparisons between cities
- Honest takes on whether Woodbury is worth the premium over Apple Valley
- Local news that tells them whether a neighborhood is on the way up or down

Both readers convert the same way:
**They find a Pemberton agent after reading content that made them trust us.**

The content earns the trust. The Pemberton CTA is the bridge.
Every post should end with a natural, non-pushy path to a Pemberton agent.

---

# CONTENT FILTERS

Before publishing any piece of content, ask:

1. **Would a relocating buyer find this genuinely useful?**
   If someone moving from Chicago to Edina read this, would they feel
   like they learned something real?

2. **Is it specific enough to be trustworthy?**
   Generic statements ("Edina has great schools") are forgettable.
   Specific ones ("Edina High School ranks in the top 5 statewide
   for graduation rates") are citable and remembered.

3. **Does it read as a resource, not a brochure?**
   We can acknowledge trade-offs. Edina is expensive. St. Cloud is far.
   Hopkins has older housing stock. Honesty builds trust faster than spin.

4. **Does it point naturally to a Pemberton agent?**
   Not in a salesy way. In the way a knowledgeable friend would say:
   "When you're ready to actually look at homes, I know the right people."

---

# VOICE AND TONE

**Informative, not promotional.** We are a resource first.
The Pemberton connection is real and disclosed — it doesn't need to be hidden,
but it also shouldn't dominate every paragraph.

**Specific over general.** Name the school. State the median price.
Give the commute time. The specifics are what make people bookmark us
and come back.

**Honest about trade-offs.** Every city has something that's not perfect.
Acknowledging it builds credibility. Ignoring it reads like a brochure.

**Local-voice authority.** Write as if the author has lived in or
consistently worked in these communities. Not as a national content farm
guessing at local details.
